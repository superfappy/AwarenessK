EVIDENCE 01 — THE SENDER

Look at the address. Read it letter by letter.

m365-alerts@kitopi-it.co
.co — NOT .com
Looks like kitopi-it.co
Real domain kitopi.com

The brand name is the bait. The suffix is the truth. Trust ends at the dot.

EVIDENCE 02 — THE CLOCK

PRESSURE 04:00:00

Real Microsoft alerts impose no deadline. Urgency is not policy — it is a manipulation tactic.

EVIDENCE 03 — THE GEOGRAPHY

A sign-in from somewhere far — chosen precisely because it is far.

The location is bait — designed to scare you, not inform you. A familiar city would never trigger the click.

FIELD MANUAL · 4 PROTOCOLS

Habits that defeat every fear-phish.

  1. I.

    PAUSE — sixty seconds.

    If the message will not survive a one-minute pause, it was never meant to.

  2. II.

    READ — letter by letter.

    Trust the suffix, not the brand. .co ≠ .com

  3. III.

    VERIFY — go direct.

    Open a new tab. Type portal.office.com by hand. The truth is in your sign-in log.

  4. IV.

    REPORT — always.

    One report can protect a thousand colleagues. Reporting is the work.

PROTOCOL IV · IN PRACTICE

How to report a phish at Kitopi.

01

Use Outlook's Report Message → Phishing.

In Outlook (desktop or web), open the suspicious message, click the Report icon in the toolbar, and choose Phishing. This sends the original headers and content directly to InfoSec for analysis.
